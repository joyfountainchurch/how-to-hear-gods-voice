**A Foundation For Engaging The Supernatural Realms Of God**

Martin Luther, a famous German monk, sparked the Reformation in 1517 by challenging the Catholic Church's practices, particularly the sale of indulgences, through his "95 Theses." This movement led to the establishment of Protestantism and significant changes in Christian practices across Europe. As a principal actor in the reformation, he taught on the subject of "the priesthood of every believer." 

This was a truth kept from the church, based on the belief that the clergy (priests) were learned, while the people, the laity, relied on the priests for the interpretation of scripture.

Ray Stedman of Authentic Christianity captures it brilliantly: "In those days, the church taught that there was a body of men set aside to be a special group of priests who acted as a kind of intermediary between God and the rest of ordinary men, but Martin Luther exploded that idea. He taught from the Scriptures that there is no such body at all but that every person who comes to know Jesus Christ is constituted a priest under God and that he joins with the great high priest, Jesus Christ himself, in a ministry of mercy, blessing, and service to a world which is fragmented and bleeding and broken, that this is *our* ministry, this is the calling to which God has called *us*. That idea hit with radical impact in Europe as people began to catch on to the exciting adventure of being this kind of a priest unto God."

Moses, a Levite, was a priest, as was his brother Aaron. Jesus Christ was also called our Chief High Priest \- Paul says Jesus is the perfect Mediator between God and man. Isn't that remarkable? It therefore follows that we are to walk in the footsteps of our High Priest. **Hebrews 5:1-6**

**The Essential Work Of A Priest**

1. A priest is a MEDIATOR between God and all people.  
2. A priest first offers sacrifices for himself, then for others.  
3. A priest serves as an intermediary between God and His people, interceding first for their SPIRITUAL NEEDS. Applying this in New Testament practice, we are intercessors on behalf of one another. 

We, as priests, also serve as intermediaries between the nonbelieving (lost) world and God the Father, who wants all to be saved. Paul's message to Timothy captures this brilliantly in **1 Timothy 2:1-10**

**1 Timothy 2:1-10 (NKJV)**

**Open Visions And Trances**

Open visions are visions granted to you while you are wide awake. On the other hand, trances occur in a semi-conscious state. Open visions are typically perceived as physical realities, whereas a trance involves entering an altered state and a deeper immersion in a spiritual experience.

**Interpretation Of Dreams**

Not all dreams are important. The scriptures teach that some dreams come from our busyness during the day or in the course of our activities in life (Ecclesiastes 5:3): "For a dream comes through much activity, And a fool's voice is known by his many words." Some dreams have a direct word of knowledge, as with Paul and the Macedonian man in a dream, so a great deal of interpretation is not needed.

To interpret the dreams that grip your attention, ask for wisdom. Joseph and Daniel are two great examples of that. The Bible says that if we ask for wisdom, we will receive it from God. The Father will not grant you a vision of the night to confuse you. Remember, He is not the author of confusion.
