**Divine Patterns (Repetition From A Relationship)**

In Session 3, we talked about how, “as we live out our lives as priests unto our God, minding our priestly business, the natural should flow at some point into the supernatural." Don’t ever forget that\! In the same way, the Bible is a book where, if we intentionally read and meditate on it, we will walk into the supernatural in varied degrees. Why? Because it is authored by the Lord Himself. The scriptures are where we find “observable and learnable” patterns of God’s dealings with humanity. It is in those pages that we observe and learn patterns for ourselves. The scriptures will help you “do you” in God’s will.

In Apostle Peter’s letter to the early Jewish believers, he shows that scripture is a supernatural product. **2 Peter 1:20-21**

**“**Knowing this first, that no prophecy of Scripture is of any private interpretation, for prophecy never came by the will of man, but holy men of God spoke *as they were* moved by the Holy Spirit.”

In 2 Timothy 3:16, Apostle Paul writes to Timothy, his mentee, whom he aptly called “my son in the faith,” declaring that God Himself authored scripture.

“All Scripture *is* given by inspiration of God, and *is* profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness.” 

Don’t you just like the word INSTRUCTION there? What do scientists look for to establish a principle? Well, you probably guessed it: PATTERNS\! What do programmers or developers seek to accomplish with programmable language? Yes, you got it again: PATTERNS\! Art is a compendium of what? PATTERNS\! Whether it be fine arts, musical arts, drama, etc. We are always looking at PATTERNS\! The weather, as much as it influences our lives, is described in what? “Weather Patterns.” 

Here is a description of how patterns work with programmers from a 1994 work authored by the “Gang of Four” (Erich Gamma, Richard Helm, Ralph Johnson, and John Vlissides) titled “***Design Patterns: Elements of Reusable Object-Oriented Software”*** (1994)

“A software design pattern describes a reusable solution to a commonly needed behaviour in software. A design pattern is not a rigid structure to be copied directly into source code. Rather, it is a description and template for solving a particular type of problem that can be used across many different contexts, including programming languages and computing platforms. Design patterns are formalised best practices that programmers can use to solve common problems in software design.”

And here is the statement of all statements: In **John 5:39,** Jesus Himself, the Author and Finisher of our faith, our Master, Kinsman Redeemer and Saviour, points unequivocally to the supernatural nature of the scriptures, because they testify of Him.

**“**You search the Scriptures, for in them you think you have eternal life; and these are they which testify of Me. But you are not willing to come to Me that you may have life.”

**Lessons From Samuel The Young Trainee Priest/Prophet (Repetition From A Relationship)**

Samuel was born to Hannah and her husband Elkanah. Hannah had long been barren and suffered deeply because of it. In her anguish, she fervently prayed to the Lord at the tabernacle in Shiloh, vowing that if God granted her a son, she would dedicate him to the Lord's service for his entire life.

God heard her prayer and answered it. Hannah conceived and gave birth to a son, whom she named Samuel, meaning "heard by God," because the Lord had listened to her plea.  
True to her vow, once Samuel was weaned, Hannah brought him to the tabernacle and presented him to the priest Eli, entrusting him to live there and serve the Lord.

Let us read **1 Samuel 3:1-21**

**1 Samuel 3:1-21 (NKJV)**

"Now the boy Samuel ministered to the Lord before Eli. And the word of the Lord was rare in those days; there was no widespread revelation. And it came to pass at that time, while Eli was lying down in his place, and when his eyes had begun to grow so dim that he could not see, and before the lamp of God went out in the tabernacle of the Lord where the ark of God was, and while Samuel was lying down, that the Lord called Samuel. And he answered, 'Here I am!' So he ran to Eli and said, 'Here I am, for you called me.' And he said, 'I did not call; lie down again.' And he went and lay down.

Samuel was quickly learning a pattern in his life. This developed to the point where hearing God came with ease. It started with him hearing a voice calling out to him: Samuel, Samuel. It concretised with him paying attention and saying, “Speak, Lord, for your servant hears.” Eli had to coach him on how to handle the repeated pattern. I am working with you in a similar manner today. What are the patterns you are finding in your own life?

My Example: Years ago, I found that around September to October every year, I start getting a strong pull within my spirit to retreat.
