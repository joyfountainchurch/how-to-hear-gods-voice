A Foundation For Engaging The Supernatural Realms Of God

Martin Luther, a famous German monk, sparked the Reformation in 1517 by challenging the Catholic Church's practices, particularly the sale of indulgences, through his "95 Theses."

He taught on the subject of "the priesthood of every believer."

This was a truth kept from the church, based on the belief that the clergy (priests) were learned, while the people, the laity, relied on the priests for the interpretation of scripture.

Ray Stedman, Authentic Christianity

"Martin Luther exploded that idea. He taught from the Scriptures that there is no such body at all but that every person who comes to know Jesus Christ is constituted a priest under God and that he joins with the great high priest, Jesus Christ himself, in a ministry of mercy, blessing, and service to a world which is fragmented and bleeding and broken, that this is our ministry, this is the calling to which God has called us."

Moses, a Levite, was a priest, as was his brother Aaron. Jesus Christ was also called our Chief High Priest — Paul says Jesus is the perfect Mediator between God and man.

It therefore follows that we are to walk in the footsteps of our High Priest. Hebrews 5:1-6

WE ARE PRIESTS UNTO OUR GOD.

The Essential Work Of A Priest

  1. A priest is a MEDIATOR between God and all people.
  2. A priest first offers sacrifices for himself, then for others.
  3. A priest serves as an intermediary between God and His people, interceding first for their SPIRITUAL NEEDS.

We, as priests, also serve as intermediaries between the nonbelieving (lost) world and God the Father, who wants all to be saved.

Paul's message to Timothy captures this brilliantly in 1 Timothy 2:1-10

Open Visions And Trances

Open visions are granted to you while you are wide awake. Trances occur in a semi-conscious state.

Open visions are typically perceived as physical realities, whereas a trance involves entering an altered state and a deeper immersion in a spiritual experience.

Zacharias had an open vision while performing his duties. The supernatural realm opened up to him, and the angel Gabriel was right there before him.

Interpretation Of Dreams

Not all dreams are important. The scriptures teach that some dreams come from our busyness during the day or in the course of our activities in life.

"For a dream comes through much activity, And a fool's voice is known by his many words." — Ecclesiastes 5:3

To interpret the dreams that grip your attention, ask for wisdom. Joseph and Daniel are two great examples of that.

The Bible says that if we ask for wisdom, we will receive it from God. The Father will not grant you a vision of the night to confuse you.

Remember, He is not the author of confusion.
