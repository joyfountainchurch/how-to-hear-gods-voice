Hearing God's Voice

Session 1: Introduction and Overview

John 10:5, 27-28 (NKJV)

5 "Yet they will by no means follow a stranger, but will flee from him, for they do not know the voice of strangers."

27 "My sheep hear My voice, and I know them, and they follow Me.

28 And I give them eternal life, and they shall never perish; neither shall anyone snatch them out of My hand."

Course Objective

The purpose of this course is to help participants discern the voice of the Father in 5 sessions of an hour each.

Expected Outcome

After completion, you should have clarity in knowing when the Lord is speaking to you in whatever way He chooses to speak.

You will also learn to hear The Father's Voice for yourself first, before hearing Him for others.

Introduction

The voice of God has always been heard from creation and throughout scripture. His voice is valid today as it was then.

  • An Angel was sent to Mary to announce the Divine purpose for her life
  • Joseph was informed in a dream to take Mary as his wife
  • An Angel warned the wise men in a dream not to return to Herod

If the birth of the Saviour was surrounded by God's voice distinctively punctuating the events... And if the volume of scriptures show humans hearing clearly from the God of heaven... Why would it be strange for the Lord to speak to us today?

4 Principal Ways The Lord Speaks To Us

You cannot miss His voice if you're paying attention or seeking to hear Him.

1. Audible Voice

  • An actual voice giving you instructions

2. An Inner Witness In Your Spirit

  • A voice or knowing within your spirit guiding you and confirming things to you

3. Through The Written Text

  • Why? Because Scripture is God-breathed!
  • Scripture text illumined by the Spirit

4. Through Third Parties

  • Angels (Luke 1:26-38, Daniel 9:1-27)
  • Other human agents: Ananias, Agabus
  • Preachers and ministers of God
  • Other believers sharing their faith
  • God can use anything to send a message to you—including inanimate objects

The Big Question Many Ask

Why do some people struggle to hear even when they pray to hear God speak to them? They say God is not speaking to them.

Could the problem be personal pride, an unrepentant heart, idol worship? Or could it be that they haven't been well taught/discipled?
