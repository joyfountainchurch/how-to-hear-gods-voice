Hearing God's Voice

Session 5: The Father Initiates Conversation

One thing you will come across in how God speaks to His people is that He does initiate conversations.

You also, as His child, can and should initiate conversations.

Our God is "the same yesterday, today, and forever."

Abraham

The Lord initiated a conversation with Abraham.

Genesis 18:16-33

Genesis 18:16-21 (NKJV)

16-17 Then the men rose from there and looked toward Sodom, and Abraham went with them to send them on the way. And the Lord said, "Shall I hide from Abraham what I am doing,

18-19 since Abraham shall surely become a great and mighty nation, and all the nations of the earth shall be blessed in him? For I have known him, in order that he may command his children and his household after him, that they keep the way of the Lord, to do righteousness and justice, that the Lord may bring to Abraham what He has spoken to him."

20-21 And the Lord said, "Because the outcry against Sodom and Gomorrah is great, and because their sin is very grave, I will go down now and see whether they have done altogether according to the outcry against it that has come to Me; and if not, I will know."

Genesis 18:22-26 (NKJV)

22-23 Then the men turned away from there and went toward Sodom, but Abraham still stood before the Lord. And Abraham came near and said, "Would You also destroy the righteous with the wicked?

24-25 Suppose there were fifty righteous within the city; would You also destroy the place and not spare it for the fifty righteous that were in it? Far be it from You to do such a thing as this... Shall not the Judge of all the earth do right?"

26 So the Lord said, "If I find in Sodom fifty righteous within the city, then I will spare all the place for their sakes."

Genesis 18:27-33 (NKJV)

27-28 Then Abraham answered and said, "Indeed now, I who am but dust and ashes have taken it upon myself to speak to the Lord: Suppose there were five less than the fifty righteous; would You destroy all of the city for lack of five?" So He said, "If I find there forty-five, I will not destroy it."

29-31 "Suppose forty?" — "I will not do it." "Suppose thirty?" — "I will not do it." "Suppose twenty?" — "I will not destroy it."

32-33 "Suppose ten should be found there?" And He said, "I will not destroy it for the sake of ten." So the Lord went His way as soon as He had finished speaking with Abraham; and Abraham returned to his place.

Starting Off With Scriptures

The Holy Scriptures: Daniel understood through the books. Jesus understood through the books. Nehemiah understood through what he heard and the books he had studied.

You Can and Should Initiate Conversations

While the Father initiates conversation anytime, anywhere, as He wills, you're more than welcome to talk to your Father just as you would to someone standing right by you.

In any best-friend relationship, creating a special time to meet and enjoy each other's company comes naturally.

In the Garden before the fall, the Lord God met Adam and Eve daily for fellowship.

Redemption restores that fellowship.

What To Say To Abba

  • Ask questions. Ask Him anything.
  • Talk to Him just like if you were talking to a dear friend
  • Praise and worship Him with your words
  • Sing to Him songs of praise and worship
  • Repent. Keep short accounts.
