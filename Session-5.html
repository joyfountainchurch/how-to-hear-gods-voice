Journeying through scripture (our instructional Manual), one thing you will come across in how God speaks to His people, particularly in individual relationships such as the one He had with Abraham, is that He does initiate conversations. You also, as His child, can and should initiate conversations. We will, as usual, draw examples from the Old and New Testaments. Doing this helps us observe and establish consistency and patterns, as our God is "the same yesterday, today, and forever."

Abraham: The Lord initiated a conversation with Abraham in Genesis 18:16-33.

Genesis 18:16-33 (NKJV)

16 "Then the men rose from there and looked toward Sodom, and Abraham went with them to send them on the way. 17 And the Lord said, 'Shall I hide from Abraham what I am doing, 18 since Abraham shall surely become a great and mighty nation, and all the nations of the earth shall be blessed in him? 19 For I have known him, in order that he may command his children and his household after him, that they keep the way of the Lord, to do righteousness and justice, that the Lord may bring to Abraham what He has spoken to him.' 20 And the Lord said, 'Because the outcry against Sodom and Gomorrah is great, and because their sin is very grave, 21 I will go down now and see whether they have done altogether according to the outcry against it that has come to Me; and if not, I will know.'

22 Then the men turned away from there and went toward Sodom, but Abraham still stood before the Lord. 23 And Abraham came near and said, 'Would You also destroy the righteous with the wicked? 24 Suppose there were fifty righteous within the city; would You also destroy the place and not spare it for the fifty righteous that were in it? 25 Far be it from You to do such a thing as this, to slay the righteous with the wicked, so that the righteous should be as the wicked; far be it from You! Shall not the Judge of all the earth do right?'

26 So the Lord said, 'If I find in Sodom fifty righteous within the city, then I will spare all the place for their sakes.'

27 Then Abraham answered and said, 'Indeed now, I who am but dust and ashes have taken it upon myself to speak to the Lord: 28 Suppose there were five less than the fifty righteous; would You destroy all of the city for lack of five?' So He said, 'If I find there forty-five, I will not destroy it.'

29 And he spoke to Him yet again and said, 'Suppose there should be forty found there?' So He said, 'I will not do it for the sake of forty.'

30 Then he said, 'Let not the Lord be angry, and I will speak: Suppose thirty should be found there?' So He said, 'I will not do it if I find thirty there.'

31 And he said, 'Indeed now, I have taken it upon myself to speak to the Lord: Suppose twenty should be found there?' So He said, 'I will not destroy it for the sake of twenty.'

32 Then he said, 'Let not the Lord be angry, and I will speak but once more: Suppose ten should be found there?' And He said, 'I will not destroy it for the sake of ten.' 33 So the Lord went His way as soon as He had finished speaking with Abraham; and Abraham returned to his place."

How He Speaks | Starting Off With Scriptures

The Holy Scriptures: Daniel understood through the books (studying Jeremiah), and Jesus understood through the books. Nehemiah understood through the combination of what he heard from people and the books he had studied. Peter explained Pentecost through the books. But all these examples also included direct messages and confirmations from heaven through angels, the Holy Spirit in Jesus' case, or third-party messengers like Nehemiah.

Circumstantial Evidence

  • When things line up in an unusual way
  • You cannot miss it
  • I would not place too much emphasis on this, because circumstances may not align with the reality of our faith walk.

You Can and Should Initiate Conversations With the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

  • While the Father initiates conversation anytime, anywhere, as He wills, you're more than welcome to talk to your Father just as you would to someone standing right by you.
  • In any best-friend relationship, creating a special time to meet and enjoy each other's company comes naturally. In the Garden before the fall, the Lord God met Adam and Eve daily for fellowship. Redemption restores that fellowship.

What To Say To Abba

  • Ask questions. Ask Him anything. He will answer directly or send help. He decides, and you will know. Abraham "stood before the Lord" after the visiting angels left him, and he asked the Lord several questions about Sodom. In this case, Abraham initiated further conversation.
  • Talk to Him just like if you were talking to a dear friend.
  • Praise and worship Him with your words. Develop the habit.
  • Sing to Him songs of praise and worship
  • Repent. Keep short accounts. 1 John 1:9-10
1 John 1:9-10 (NKJV)

9 "If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness. 10 If we say that we have not sinned, we make Him a liar, and His word is not in us."
