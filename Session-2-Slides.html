Hearing God's Voice

Session 2: The Voice Of The Stranger

Jesus taught that His sheep hear His voice and that the voice of a stranger they will not hear.

He stated that we would FLEE from a stranger's voice. Amen!

John 10:1-3 (NKJV)

1 "Most assuredly, I say to you, he who does not enter the sheepfold by the door, but climbs up some other way, the same is a thief and a robber.

2 But he who enters by the door is the shepherd of the sheep.

3 To him the doorkeeper opens, and the sheep hear his voice; and he calls his own sheep by name and leads them out.

John 10:4-6 (NKJV)

4 And when he brings out his own sheep, he goes before them; and the sheep follow him, for they know his voice.

5 Yet they will by no means follow a stranger, but will flee from him, for they do not know the voice of strangers."

6 Jesus used this illustration, but they did not understand the things which He spoke to them.

The voice of a stranger is unfamiliar.

Jesus sent His Holy Spirit to teach, comfort, empower, and guide us into all truth.

Most of the time it is the voice of the Holy Spirit we hear as believers.

Who Is The Stranger?

The stranger is the devil. Jesus taught us that he comes to steal, kill, and destroy.

John 10:7-10 (NKJV)

7 Then Jesus said to them again, "Most assuredly, I say to you, I am the door of the sheep.

8 All who ever came before Me are thieves and robbers, but the sheep did not hear them.

9 I am the door. If anyone enters by Me, he will be saved, and will go in and out and find pasture.

10 The thief does not come except to steal, and to kill, and to destroy. I have come that they may have life, and that they may have it more abundantly."

Lucifer's Fall

The devil was an archangel in heaven by the name Lucifer, who lost his place due to pride and wanting the Holy Throne for himself.

Read on your own: Isaiah 14:12-15 and Ezekiel 28:11-19

The Stranger's Other Common Names

  • Thief, Father of lies, Murderer from the beginning
  • That old serpent, The dragon
  • Prince of the power of the air, Deceiver
  • The accuser, Beelzebub (lord of the flies)
  • Satan, The tempter

He is of darkness, and we are of the Light.

John 8:12 (NKJV)

Then Jesus spoke to them again, saying, "I am the light of the world. He who follows Me shall not walk in darkness, but have the light of life."

The Stranger's Cohorts

The stranger has cohorts: evil spirits (fallen angels, demons, devils), and humans who elect to serve the devil knowingly or ignorantly.

The Voice Of The Stranger

This stranger's strategy must be understood so that he does not gain an advantage over us.

2 Corinthians 2:10-11 (NKJV)

10 Now whom you forgive anything, I also forgive. For if indeed I have forgiven anything, I have forgiven that one for your sakes in the presence of Christ,

11 lest Satan should take advantage of us; for we are not ignorant of his devices.

satan operates his enterprise of deceit FIRST BY HIS VOICE.

This is what confuses many people because we are told he disguises himself as an angel of light.

We see this in the Garden of Eden.

Genesis 3:1-3 (NKJV)

2 And the woman said to the serpent, "We may eat the fruit of the trees of the garden;

3 but of the fruit of the tree which is in the midst of the garden, God has said, 'You shall not eat it, nor shall you touch it, lest you die.'"

Genesis 3:4-5 (NKJV)

5 For God knows that in the day you eat of it your eyes will be opened, and you will be like God, knowing good and evil."

Genesis 3:6-7 (NKJV)

7 Then the eyes of both of them were opened, and they knew that they were naked; and they sewed fig leaves together and made themselves coverings.

Genesis 3:8-10 (NKJV)

Genesis 3:11-13 (NKJV)

12 Then the man said, "The woman whom You gave to be with me, she gave me of the tree, and I ate."

The Lord's voice rattled the corruption of the stranger's voice they had listened to.

The stranger's voice aims to deceive, subvert, and create rebellion in those who listen to him.

In warfare, you must have some knowledge about how the enemy operates in order to successfully defend and counter their attack.

We must not give any opportunity to the devil to use his voice to deceive us. We cannot afford to be ignorant of his devices.

Resisting The Stranger's Voice

We have been provided with sufficient grace to defeat the enemy.

In fact, the enemy is a defeated foe. You must remember that.

Colossians 2:11-13 (NKJV)

11 In Him you were also circumcised with the circumcision made without hands, by putting off the body of the sins of the flesh, by the circumcision of Christ,

12 buried with Him in baptism, in which you also were raised with Him through faith in the working of God, who raised Him from the dead.

13 And you, being dead in your trespasses and the uncircumcision of your flesh, He has made alive together with Him, having forgiven you all trespasses,

Colossians 2:14-15 (NKJV)

14 having wiped out the handwriting of requirements that was against us, which was contrary to us. And He has taken it out of the way, having nailed it to the cross.

15 Having disarmed principalities and powers, He made a public spectacle of them, triumphing over them in it.

James 4:7-8 (NKJV)

7 Therefore submit to God. Resist the devil and he will flee from you.

8 Draw near to God and He will draw near to you. Cleanse your hands, you sinners; and purify your hearts, you double-minded.

Eve entertained the stranger's voice through the serpent, offering a "better than this life" than what God had already provided.

The devil continues today to offer a "better than this life" through lying, covetousness, stealing, cheating, murder, adultery, fornication, and idol worship.

10 Things We Should Do
To Resist The Devil

  1. Be familiar with the Lord's voice by immersing ourselves in His word
  2. Refusing and rejecting the stranger's voice
  3. Being filled with the Holy Spirit through prayer, fasting, scripture reading, study, and meditation
  4. Submitting to one another in the fear of God
  5. Obeying the word of God

10 Things (continued)

  1. Speaking Psalms, Hymns, and spiritual songs
  2. Make church attendance a priority
  3. Ask for prayer when needed
  4. Be a worshipper of God all round (satan can't stand that)
  5. Cast out devils

Conclusion

The most important skill in our Christian walk and work is hearing and acting on the Father's voice through His Holy Spirit.

1 Samuel 3:1-6 (NKJV)

1 Now the boy Samuel ministered to the Lord before Eli. And the word of the Lord was rare in those days; there was no widespread revelation.

2-3 And it came to pass at that time, while Eli was lying down in his place, and when his eyes had begun to grow dim so that he could not see, and before the lamp of God went out in the tabernacle of the Lord where the ark of God was, and while Samuel was lying down,

4 that the Lord called Samuel. And he answered, "Here I am!"

5 So he ran to Eli and said, "Here I am, for you called me." And he said, "I did not call; lie down again." And he went and lay down.

6 Then the Lord called yet again, "Samuel!" So Samuel arose and went to Eli, and said, "Here I am, for you called me." He answered, "I did not call, my son; lie down again."

1 Samuel 3:7-11 (NKJV)

7 (Now Samuel did not yet know the Lord, nor was the word of the Lord yet revealed to him.)

8 And the Lord called Samuel again the third time. So he arose and went to Eli, and said, "Here I am, for you did call me." Then Eli perceived that the Lord had called the boy.

9 Therefore Eli said to Samuel, "Go, lie down; and it shall be, if He calls you, that you must say, 'Speak, Lord, for Your servant hears.'" So Samuel went and lay down in his place.

10 Now the Lord came and stood and called as at other times, "Samuel! Samuel!" And Samuel answered, "Speak, for Your servant hears."

11 Then the Lord said to Samuel: "Behold, I will do something in Israel at which both ears of everyone who hears it will tingle."

"We know the voice of our Lord Jesus. The voice of a stranger we will not hear."
